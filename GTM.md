# Go-To-Market Strategy (GTM)

## The Exact Target User
- **Job Title:** Fractional CFOs and VP of Engineering at Series A/B startups.
- **Why:** Fractional CFOs are actively looking for quick wins to justify their retainer by cutting burn. VPs of Engineering at Series A are suddenly dealing with procurement bloat as they scale from 10 to 40 devs and lose track of individual GitHub Copilot/Cursor seat utilization.

## Triggers: What they do right before wanting this
- A founder gets an angry Slack message from their accountant about an unexpectedly high corporate Amex bill.
- A VP of Engineering is preparing for an end-of-quarter budget review with the board and needs to show they are managing vendor sprawl.
- They search: "How to track Anthropic API spend" or "Manage developer SaaS seats."

## Where They Hang Out Online
- **Slack Communities:** `Tech Leads` (specifically the #engineering-management channel) and `Fractional Execs`.
- **Subreddits:** `r/SaaS` (founders complaining about bills) and `r/devops` (where infrastructure costs are discussed).
- **X (Twitter):** Lists tracking "Build in Public" founders and tech-focused accountants/bookkeepers.

## Acquiring the First 100 Users ($0 Budget)
1. **The "Audit Teardown" Thread:** I will manually run the audit tool on the public, known stack of a prominent startup (e.g., "How much is Vercel overpaying for AI?"). I'll post the teardown on X, tagging their engineering leaders, and link the tool at the end. 
2. **Cold Outreach to Fractional CFOs:** I will scrape LinkedIn for "Fractional CFO SaaS" and send a direct offer: *"Run this free tool on your clients' stacks. It takes 2 minutes, makes you look like a hero for finding 15% savings, and costs you nothing."* This leverages their existing client base for distribution.
3. **IndieHackers / Hacker News "Show HN":** Position the tool not as a B2B product, but as an open-source-adjacent "developer protection tool" against predatory SaaS pricing minimums. 

## The Unfair Distribution Channel
**Accounting Firm Partnerships:** Credex can partner with startup-focused bookkeeping firms (like Pilot or Kruze Consulting). The firm runs the AI Spend Audit for all their clients during monthly reconciliation. The firm looks smart, the startup saves money, and Credex gets highly qualified, pre-vetted leads in bulk without spending a dollar on direct ads.

## Week-1 Traction Goals
- 400 unique page views from X and HN.
- 60 audits run to completion (15% conversion).
- 12 high-intent leads captured (>$500/mo savings identified).
- 3 Credex consultations booked.
