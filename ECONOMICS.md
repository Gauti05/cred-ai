# Unit Economics

## Value of a Converted Lead
- **Assumption:** A high-intent lead represents a startup currently spending >$2,000/mo on AI infrastructure.
- **Credex Margin:** If Credex offers a 20% discount on retail credits, and Credex's gross margin on the backend arbitrage is 10%, Credex earns ~$200/month per converted lead in pure profit.
- **LTV:** Assuming a 12-month retention before the startup pivots or outgrows the credits: LTV = **$2,400**.

## Cost to Acquire a Customer (CAC)
- **Direct Traffic / Social (Organic):** $0
- **Cold Email Outreach (Apollo/Instantly):** ~$150 per booked meeting (cost of tools + labor time). 
- **Partnership Channel (Bookkeepers):** $0 upfront, but requires a 10% revenue share on the margin, reducing LTV to $2,160.

## Conversion Funnel Targets for Profitability
To make this lead-gen tool a net-positive engine, we need these minimum conversion rates based on a 1,000 visitor cohort:
- **Visitor → Audit Completed:** 20% (200 audits)
- **Audit Completed → Email Captured:** 15% (30 leads)
- **Email Captured → Meets >$500 Savings Threshold:** 40% (12 qualified leads)
- **Qualified Lead → Books Consultation:** 25% (3 meetings)
- **Meeting → Credit Purchase:** 33% (1 closed deal)

*Result:* 1,000 visitors = 1 closed deal ($2,400 LTV). If our blended CAC (hosting + time + email tools) stays below $500 per 1,000 visitors, the unit economics are highly profitable.

## Path to $1M ARR in 18 Months
To hit $1M ARR via this tool alone, Credex needs roughly 416 active customers ($2,400 ARR each).
*   At our funnel conversion rate (1 customer per 1,000 visitors), we need 416,000 total visitors over 18 months (~23,000/month).
*   **What must be true:** Organic social alone won't scale to 23k/mo. The "Unfair Distribution Channel" (Accountant partnerships) must be the primary driver, where a single accounting firm partner brings 50 qualified startups at once, bypassing the top-of-funnel web traffic entirely.
